# Competitive Research Guidelines

This document provides guidance on conducting thorough competitive research for battle cards.

## Research Methodology

### Phase 1: Product Understanding (Per Product)
For each product being analyzed, gather:

1. **Core Product Information**
   - Official product website
   - Product documentation
   - Pricing pages
   - Feature lists

2. **Market Positioning**
   - Target audience and use cases
   - Key value propositions
   - Market segment focus

3. **Technical Capabilities**
   - Core features and functionality
   - Integrations and ecosystem
   - Technical limitations or requirements
   - Platform/infrastructure details

### Phase 2: Comparative Intelligence

1. **Third-Party Reviews**
   - G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Gartner
   - User reviews (focus on recent ones)
   - Analyst reports
   - Industry comparisons

2. **Customer Sentiment**
   - Common praise themes
   - Common complaints or pain points
   - Feature requests
   - Comparison threads (Reddit, forums, Stack Overflow)

3. **Competitive Comparisons**
   - Search for "[Product A] vs [Product B]" articles
   - Look for migration stories
   - Identify common comparison criteria

### Phase 3: Market Context

1. **Recent Developments**
   - Recent product updates or launches
   - Funding announcements
   - Strategic partnerships
   - Market momentum indicators

2. **Industry Trends**
   - Emerging requirements in the space
   - Technology shifts
   - Buyer preference changes

## Information Priority

### Must-Have Information
- Core product features and capabilities
- Pricing model and starting prices
- Target customer segments
- 2-3 key differentiators
- Known strengths and weaknesses

### Nice-to-Have Information
- Detailed technical specifications
- Customer testimonials
- Market share data
- Roadmap information
- Team size and company details

### Be Pragmatic
- If information isn't publicly available, note it as "Not publicly available"
- Don't speculate on missing data
- Focus on verifiable information
- Prioritize recent information over outdated data

## Research Quality Checks

Before completing research:
- [ ] Verified information from primary sources where possible
- [ ] Checked multiple sources for key claims
- [ ] Noted any information that may be outdated
- [ ] Identified gaps in available information
- [ ] Prioritized features relevant to user's specified attributes

## Web Search Strategy

### Effective Search Queries
Use varied search approaches:
- "[Product name] features"
- "[Product name] pricing"
- "[Product name] vs [Competitor]"
- "[Product name] reviews"
- "[Product name] pros and cons"
- "Why companies choose [Product name]"
- "[Product name] limitations"
- "[Product name] alternatives"

### Source Prioritization
1. **Primary sources** (highest value)
   - Official product websites
   - Official documentation
   - Company blogs

2. **Authoritative third-party sources**
   - Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester)
   - Review platforms (G2, Capterra)
   - Tech publications

3. **Community sources**
   - Discussion forums
   - Social media
   - User reviews

## Attribution and Transparency

- Always cite sources for specific claims
- Note information recency (e.g., "As of Q3 2024...")
- Flag information gaps clearly
- Distinguish between facts and interpretation
