# Battle Card Template

This document defines the standard structure for competitive battle cards.

## Battle Card Sections

### 1. Executive Summary
- Brief overview (2-3 sentences)
- Key positioning statement
- Primary competitive advantage

### 2. Product Overview
- **Our Product**: Core value proposition, target audience, key use cases
- **Competitor Product**: Same structure for comparison

### 3. Head-to-Head Comparison
Quick reference table format:
- Feature/Attribute Name
- Our Product Status/Value
- Competitor Status/Value
- Advantage Indicator (✓, ✗, ~)

### 4. Competitive Positioning

#### Our Strengths vs Competitor
- List 3-5 key advantages with supporting evidence
- Include metrics, customer testimonials, or market data where available

#### Their Strengths (Be Honest)
- List 2-3 areas where competitor has advantages
- Prepare counter-positioning for sales conversations

#### Neutralize Their Strengths
- For each competitor strength, provide:
  - Reframing strategy
  - Counter-narrative
  - Questions to ask prospects

### 5. Win/Loss Themes

#### When We Win
- Common scenarios where our product prevails
- Customer profiles that favor our solution
- Buying criteria that work in our favor

#### When We Lose
- Scenarios to watch out for
- Customer profiles that may prefer competitor
- How to avoid or pivot in these situations

### 6. Pricing Comparison
- Pricing model overview (subscription, usage-based, one-time, etc.)
- Starting price points
- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) considerations
- Value-based positioning around price

### 7. Key Differentiators
Top 3-5 unique selling propositions that matter most:
- What makes our product truly different
- Why it matters to customers
- Supporting evidence or proof points

### 8. Handling Common Objections

For each common objection:
- **Objection**: "Competitor X has feature Y"
- **Response**: Reframe and provide alternative narrative
- **Proof Point**: Evidence, customer story, or data to support response

### 9. Discovery Questions
Questions to ask prospects to:
- Uncover pain points our product solves
- Reveal requirements that favor our solution
- Identify gaps in competitor offerings

### 10. Resources & Proof Points
- Case studies relevant to this competitive scenario
- Demo scripts or videos
- Third-party reviews or analyst reports
- ROI calculators or comparison tools
- Customer testimonials specific to competitive wins

## Output Format Guidelines

### For Markdown Battle Cards
- Use clear hierarchical headers (##, ###)
- Use tables for feature comparisons
- Use bullet points for lists
- Bold key terms and advantages
- Use callout sections for critical insights

### For Excel Battle Cards
Sheet structure:
1. **Summary** sheet: Executive summary and key takeaways
2. **Feature Comparison** sheet: Detailed feature-by-feature matrix
3. **Pricing** sheet: Pricing models and TCO analysis
4. **Win/Loss** sheet: Win/loss themes and scenarios
5. **Objections** sheet: Common objections and responses
6. **Resources** sheet: Links to supporting materials
